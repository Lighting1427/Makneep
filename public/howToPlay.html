How to Play Makneep

Board and Piece Setup

The Makneep board consists of 64 squares arranged in an 8x8 grid. Each player has 8 pieces, totaling 16 pieces. The pieces are arranged on the bottom-most row of each player's side, directly opposite each other, filling all 8 squares from left to right.

Rules of Movement

1. Players can move only one piece at a time.
2. Pieces can move vertically or horizontally in four directions: up, down, left, and right, with no limit to the number of squares. However, they must move straight along the grid lines.

Movement Restrictions
Pieces cannot move diagonally or jump over other pieces. Movement must stop immediately when blocked by another piece.

Rules for Capturing Opponent's Pieces

Capturing opponent pieces can be done in the following ways:

  • Method 1: Use one piece to capture two opponent pieces; captured pieces are immediately removed.
  • Method 2: Use 2-6 pieces to capture two opponent pieces; the captured pieces are removed immediately.
  • Method 3: Capture occurs when pieces trap an opponent piece from both sides along the same line.

Winning Conditions

Players can win in three ways:

  • Condition 1: Capture all opponent pieces; the player who captures all opponent pieces wins.
  • Condition 2: Trap the opponent so they cannot move; the trapped player loses.
  • Condition 3: If time runs out, the player with the most pieces remaining wins. If tied, the game is a draw.
